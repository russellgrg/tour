Top Places to Visit

Paro Taktsang (Tiger’s Nest Monastery)

One of Bhutan’s most iconic landmarks, dramatically perched on a cliff 3,000 meters above sea level. Built in 1692, it is said to be the site where Guru Rinpoche, who introduced Buddhism to Bhutan, meditated in the 8th century. The complex includes four main temples and several smaller ones, connected by pathways carved into the rock, offering breathtaking views of the Paro Valley. It is an important pilgrimage site that symbolizes Bhutan’s spiritual heritage.

Punakha Dzong

Also known as the “Palace of Great Happiness,” is the second oldest and one of the most beautiful dzongs in Bhutan. Situated at the confluence of the Pho Chhu and Mo Chhu rivers, it boasts intricate architecture and is surrounded by lush jacaranda trees that bloom in the spring. This dzong has played a key role in Bhutanese history, serving as the venue for royal events and being the winter residence of the central monastic body.

Thimphu’s Weekend Market

Open from Friday to Sunday, the market is where locals gather to buy and sell a variety of goods, including fresh produce, handicrafts, spices, and traditional clothing. It is not only a place to shop but also an opportunity to interact with friendly vendors and immerse yourself in the daily life of Thimphu residents.
